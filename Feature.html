Ilocos Sur

A historic province in the Ilocos Region (Region I) of northern Luzon, Philippines. Known as the “Heritage Province of the North,” it is famous for its rich culture, Spanish colonial architecture, and scenic coastal and mountain landscapes. Its capital is Vigan City, a UNESCO World Heritage Site recognized for its well-preserved Spanish-era houses and cobblestone streets. The province is divided into two districts: the First District, which includes Vigan and other heritage towns, and the Second District, home to beaches, farms, and mountain ranges. Ilocos Sur’s culture is a unique blend of Ilocano, Spanish, and Chinese influences, visible in its traditions, festivals, and cuisine. Popular delicacies include Vigan longganisa (garlic sausage), bagnet (crispy pork), empanada, and sinanglao. Tourist attractions include Calle Crisologo in Vigan, Santiago Cove, Skyline in Quirino, and the Heritage Villages. The province also takes pride in its local crafts such as abel Iloco weaving, pottery, and woodcarving. With its mix of history, culture, and natural beauty, Ilocos Sur stands as a living showcase of the Philippines’ colonial past and vibrant traditions, while continuing to thrive in modern times.

Vigan Heritage is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Ilocos Sur, known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture and cobblestone streets. It showcases a unique blend of Filipino, Spanish, Chinese, and Mexican influences through its ancestral houses, crafts, and cuisine. Today, Vigan remains a living museum of history and culture, making it one of the most treasured heritage cities in Asia.

Santiago Cove, known as the “Boracay of the North,” is a hidden gem in Ilocos Sur with fine white sand and clear blue waters. It offers a peaceful escape where visitors can swim, snorkel, or simply enjoy the sunset by the shore. With its calm beauty and local charm, the cove is a perfect spot for relaxation and adventure.

Skyline in Quirino, Ilocos Sur is a scenic mountain highway that offers sweeping views of the Cordillera ranges. Known for its cool breeze and sea-of-clouds sunrise, it’s a favorite stop for travelers, riders, and nature lovers. With its winding roads and viewing decks, the Skyline is one of the most scenic drives in Northern Luzon.
